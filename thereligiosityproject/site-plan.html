Home page

What Is The Religiosity Project?

Religion is everywhere. It has shaped laws, started wars, built communities, and given billions of people a reason to get out of bed in the morning. And yet most of us walk around with a pretty surface-level understanding of faiths that aren't our own. The Religiosity Project is our attempt to fix that. This is a site for anyone who's ever been curious but didn't really know where to start. No background required, no prior knowledge assumed, just an open mind and a willingness to learn something new. We hope to be able to accommodate that itch to learn more. We didn't create this site to push a particular belief system or to argue that religion is good or bad. What we are interested in is the fact that faith is one of the most deeply human things there is, and it deserves to be talked about seriously and openly. We wanted something that wasn't catering exclusively to believers or dismissing religion entirely. A place where the conversation is honest, accessible, and actually interesting to read. Whether you're deeply religious, completely secular, or somewhere in the middle questioning everything, this site was built with you in mind. Maybe you grew up in one tradition and want to understand others. Maybe you saw something in the news and realized you didn't have enough context to really understand it. Any of those reasons are a great reason to be here. There's no wrong way to be curious and we hope to help you answer those questions that nag at the back of your head.

Historicity & Theology

Our Commitment to Historicity and Fair, Open discussion

One thing we take seriously here is accuracy. When we talk about the history of a religion, we want to make sure we're actually getting it right, and that means going to the right sources. We're talking to scholars who have spent their careers studying these traditions, historians who understand the full context of how these faiths developed over time, and most importantly, members of the religious communities themselves. Nobody knows a religion better than the people who actually live it. Too much religious content online is written from the outside looking in, and it shows. Too often are beliefs misrepresented for the sake of protecting one's own personal views. We believe that if you're going to talk about something as important as someone's faith and its history, you owe it to your audience and to those communities to do the work. That's the standard we hold ourselves to every time we publish a video or post on our site.

News

Why Religious News Matters

Religious news is some of the most unloved, underreported, or underrated content out there, and that's a problem. When religion does make the headlines it's almost always tied to conflict, controversy, or politics. What you rarely see is the full picture. Faith communities around the world are doing genuinely remarkable things every single day and most of it never makes the news cycle. We think that needs to change. Our news page exists to cover religion the way it actually deserves to be covered, with the same seriousness and depth you'd want for any other major force in human life. That means covering the hard stories when they come up, but it also means making sure the good ones get just as much attention. Religion has always been one of the most powerful drivers of charity, community, and positive change in the world, and we're here to make sure that story gets told too.
